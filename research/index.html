Research Interests:
  • Structure of memory:

    I am interested in understanding how our memory is structured in the form of neuronal network that enables us to make sense of our sensory inputs and prepare an appropriate response in return.

    I suspect current deep-learning paradigm might fall short of being able to replicate our memory in all its complexity. Deep-learning paradigm uses unstructured sets of active neurons that represents a very weak data structure. The nature of brute-force training through thousands of examples only results in fine-tuned connectomes that, perhaps, are weak in feature representation.

  • Autonomous agents in embedded systems:

    I am intersted in devising self-organization principles for an artificial-neural-network based agent is embedded in an environment. I am interested in bringing about a consistent integration of top-down contextuality (agent-environment interaction) and bottom-up organizing principles (how an agent dynamically organizes itself in order to meet the challenges introduced by a changing environment) for an autonomous agents.

    I am currently working towards building a simulated system that can provide a proof-of-concept of how causation works in a hierarchical system. Further details will follow when I make sufficient progress with it.

  • Nature of Gravity:

    I spend a lot of time thinking about the nature of gravitational interaction and nature of spacetime. I spent over a year during my masters thesis in trying to understand the notion of emergent phenomena in Physics. Since gravitational field equations have same conceptual status as equations of fluid mechanics, one can think of gravity as emergent phenomena like fluid mechanics.

    By taking the thermodynamic limit, one can find a consistent of gravity as an emergent phenomena. A quantum theory of gravity from such an approach is manifestly different from theory of quantized gravity. One can think of theory of quantized gravity would be similar to phonon-physics.

    I think, in order to find a conmplete theory of quantum gravity, we should investigate three main theoretical constructs, viz.,

    • Black-hole singularity
    • Big-bang singularity
    • Cosmological constant problem

    about which both gravitational and quantum theory has something to say. Thus, any theory of quantum gravity must find a resolution to the aforementioned constructs.
